01Project Overview
What is GRAM (TON)? GRAM — formerly known as Toncoin — is the native cryptocurrency of The Open Network (TON), a high-performance, BFT Proof-of-Stake Layer 1 blockchain originally designed by Pavel and Nikolai Durov (founders of Telegram) in 2018. After a legal dispute with the U.S. SEC forced Telegram to abandon the project in 2020, an independent open-source community revived the network under the TON Foundation. In May 2026, Telegram officially reclaimed control — becoming TON's largest validator under the "Make TON Great Again" (MTONGA) roadmap — and the native token was rebranded back to its original name: GRAM. TON is now the exclusive blockchain powering Telegram's entire Mini App ecosystem, giving it direct access to over 1 billion monthly active users — an unmatched distribution advantage in all of crypto.

03Inflation Model (Whitepaper)
📄 Dr. Nikolai Durov — TON Whitepaper The total supply of GRAM coins is originally limited to 5 Gigatons (5 billion). This supply grows slowly as validator rewards for producing new masterchain and shardchain blocks accumulate. Validators earn approximately 20% of their staked amount per year for diligent participation. With approximately 10% of total supply expected to be staked at any given time, the baseline inflation rate is approximately 2% per year — potentially doubling supply to ~10 billion in around 35 years. Catchain 2.0 (April 2026) increased block frequency significantly, which may elevate annual inflation from ~0.6% toward ~3.6%. Counterbalancing this: a portion of transaction fees are burned, and slashed validator stakes are also burned — creating deflationary pressure that scales with network usage and validator misbehaviour.

01Core Team
Pavel Durov
Co-Founder · Telegram CEO
𝕏 Twitter ✈ Telegram
Pavel Durov (born October 10, 1984, Leningrad) is one of the most influential figures in tech and crypto. He co-founded VKontakte (VK), Russia's largest social network, before being forced out by Kremlin-aligned investors in 2014. He then founded Telegram, which grew to over 1 billion monthly active users. In 2018, he and his brother Nikolai designed The Open Network and raised $1.7 billion in a private token sale. After the SEC forced a shutdown and $18.5M settlement in 2020, the community revived the project independently. In May 2026, Durov announced Telegram would take back direct control of TON — becoming its largest validator under the MTONGA roadmap — and rebranded the native token back to its original name: GRAM. He was arrested in Paris in August 2024 over content moderation disputes, adding ongoing regulatory risk to the project's independence.
Nikolai Durov
Co-Founder · Chief Architect
Nikolai Durov is the technical genius behind The Open Network. A mathematician and programmer of extraordinary ability, he holds a PhD and is widely regarded as one of the most brilliant engineers in the blockchain space. The TON whitepaper is formally attributed to Dr. Nikolai Durov and covers the entire architecture: the Infinite Sharding Paradigm, BFT Proof-of-Stake consensus (Catchain protocol), Instant Hypercube Routing, the TON Virtual Machine (TVM), Fift programming language, and the payment channel architecture. He co-founded both VKontakte and Telegram with Pavel, and designed the MTProto encryption protocol used by Telegram. He maintains an extremely low public profile with no active social media — letting his code speak for itself.
Steve Yun
President · Founding Member
𝕏 Twitter
Steve Yun serves as President of the TON Foundation and is one of its founding members. He has been instrumental in building TON's institutional credibility and securing its exclusive partnership with Telegram. Under his leadership, the Foundation navigated the critical transition from a community-led project to a Telegram-aligned blockchain ecosystem. Yun oversaw major milestones including the USDT launch on TON (April 2024), the exclusive Mini App blockchain designation (January 2025), and institutional fundraising from Sequoia Capital, Pantera, and Benchmark. His active presence on X and track record of delivery make him one of the most important operational figures in the GRAM ecosystem.
Andrew Rogozov
Founding Member
𝕏 Twitter
Andrew Rogozov joined the TON Foundation as a founding member in January 2022, bringing an extensive background in tech leadership. He previously served as CEO of VK (VKontakte) — the same company where he and Pavel Durov first worked together — giving him a unique shared history with the project's origins. At the TON Foundation, Rogozov leads product strategy and ecosystem development, leveraging his experience scaling one of the largest social platforms in Eastern Europe. His deep familiarity with both the Durov brothers and Telegram's product-led growth philosophy makes him a culturally and strategically aligned member of the founding team.

📄 Whitepaper Tech Deep Dive — Dr. Nikolai Durov TON's architecture is among the most sophisticated in blockchain. The Infinite Sharding Paradigm treats each account as its own shardchain in the extreme case — enabling near-unlimited horizontal scaling. The Masterchain coordinates all workchains and shardchains, storing validator sets, sharding parameters, and hashes of all recent blocks. Instant Hypercube Routing ensures cross-shard messages are delivered efficiently regardless of sharding depth. The self-healing vertical blockchain mechanism allows invalid blocks to be replaced without forking the horizontal chain. The TVM natively supports Merkle proof operations — an architectural decision made at the design stage to future-proof the payment channel layer. Catchain 2.0 (April 2026) delivered approximately 6x faster block production, bringing block time down to sub-400ms finality.
